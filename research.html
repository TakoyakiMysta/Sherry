Study and Research

Our findings further suggest that time and effort management and psychological flexibility are closely related and appear to go hand in hand and, thus, both need to be considered when the aim is to reduce procrastination.

If procrastinators are more likely to play video games, and have a stronger need for immediate reward, then one would expect that many hours of video gaming and a high degree of delay discounting is common among procrastinators.

Ecomonists call this delay disconting- the longer you have to wait for a reward, the less it is worth for you. Even small delays can dramatically lower the perceived value.
